Slide 1: Introduction

Real-World Problem: Energy consumption in large buildings accounts for 40% of global energy use. Optimizing this usage is crucial for sustainability.

Energy Distribution in Commercial Buildings

Objective: Use matrix algebra to model and optimize energy usage patterns, reducing consumption by 20-30% while maintaining comfort levels.

Slide 2: Mathematical Framework

We represent the building as a system of interconnected zones:

Energy Flow Matrix (E):
E = [eij] where eij represents energy transfer from zone i to zone j
E = \begin{bmatrix} 0.8 & 0.1 & 0.05 \\ 0.05 & 0.7 & 0.15 \\ 0.02 & 0.08 & 0.9 \\ \end{bmatrix}

Each row sums to 1 (or less due to losses), representing conservation of energy principles.

Zone Interconnection Model

Three main zones: Office Spaces (40%), Common Areas (35%), Service Areas (25%)

Matrix algebra helps us understand energy flow between these interconnected zones.

Slide 3: Optimization Model

Using linear algebra to minimize energy consumption:

Objective Function: Minimize CTX
Constraints: AX ≤ B, X ≥ 0
Where:
• X = [x₁, x₂, x₃]T (energy allocation vector)
• C = cost coefficients
• A = constraint matrix
• B = maximum allowable consumption

This is a linear programming problem solvable using matrix operations:

Minimize: [0.12, 0.08, 0.15] · [x₁, x₂, x₃]T

Subject to:
\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0.3 & 0.2 \\ 0.2 & 1 & 0.4 \\ 0.1 & 0.2 & 1 \\ \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x₁ \\ x₂ \\ x₃ \end{bmatrix} ≤ \begin{bmatrix} 100 \\ 80 \\ 60 \end{bmatrix}

Slide 4: Eigenvalue Analysis

Finding steady-state energy distribution using eigenvalues:

For the energy flow matrix E, we solve:
E·v = λ·v
The eigenvector corresponding to λ=1 gives the steady-state distribution

Steady-State Energy Distribution

Office spaces can reduce consumption from 65% to 45% at steady-state

The dominant eigenvector reveals optimal redistribution of energy resources across building zones.

Slide 5: Results & Savings

Implementation of the matrix-based optimization yields:

  • 28.5% reduction in overall energy consumption
  • 19.2% cost savings on monthly energy bills
  • Improved thermal comfort in 92% of occupied spaces
  • Payback period: 2.3 years for implementation costs
Annual Savings Calculation:
Total Energy Saved = (Initial Consumption) × (Reduction Percentage)
= 250,000 kWh × 0.285 = 71,250 kWh/year
Cost Savings = 71,250 kWh × ₹8/kWh = ₹570,000/year

Carbon Footprint Reduction

Equivalent to removing 12 cars from roads annually or planting 1,200 trees
